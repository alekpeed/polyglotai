Tone Guide — sing the meaning

In Thai and Vietnamese the same syllable means different things depending on the pitch melody you sing it on. Read each tone as a little contour on a staff — low at the bottom, high at the top — the way you'd read a note. Match the shape with your ear, not a rulebook.

🎧 Listen-and-repeat · ungraded by design — no mic, nothing to get wrong. In the app, ▶ plays a native reference to shadow.
Staff: bottom = low pitch · top = high pitch ● where the note starts ✕ glottal catch (voice breaks) ▪ hard stop (cut off short)

Thai5 tones · th-TH

Central / Bangkok. One syllable khaa carries all five — see the overlay below.

คา · ข่า · ค่า · ค้า · ขา

The classic proof: one sound, khaa, sung five ways — five unrelated words. Overlaid on one staff, they're five melodies.

Vietnamese6 tones · vi-VN

Northern / Hanoi. Two of the six (ngã, nặng) break the note with a glottal catch — English never does this.

ma · mà · má · mả · mã · mạ

The famous six-way set: ma sung six ways — ghost, but, cheek, grave, horse, rice-seedling.
